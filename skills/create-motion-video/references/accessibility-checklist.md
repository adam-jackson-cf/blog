# Accessibility Checklist

Use this checklist while building the motion contract and while reviewing previews.

- Honor reduced-motion preferences by simplifying or shortening motion rather than ignoring the request.
- Keep motion purposeful; do not animate for decoration alone.
- Prefer transform and opacity changes over layout-thrashing movement.
- Keep captions readable with adequate contrast and stable line breaks.
- Keep text density low enough that the scene can be read without pausing.
- Avoid rapid flashing, aggressive zooms, or abrupt direction changes.
- Give the viewer enough dwell time on labels, diagrams, and comparison states.
- Ensure the final video still explains the concept when motion is reduced.
