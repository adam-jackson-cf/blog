# Step 2 Workflow: Build Design Contract

## Objective

Turn the local design references and Factory-inspired guidance into an explicit implementation contract for the article detail page and feed treatment.

## Required actions

1. Inspect `assets/design.png`, `assets/design2.png`, `assets/factory-missions-hero.png`, `assets/factory-missions-practice.png`, and `assets/factory-missions-how-to-use.png`.
2. Define the layout contract as the shared site header with section-level article-page hero copy, plus a narrow left rail that starts with the back link and continues into the table of contents beside the main reading column.
3. Define the feed-item contract as the standard collapsible research-feed entry pattern with the `Article` row expanded by default.
4. Define typography, accent, rules, pills, callouts, navigation behavior, justified body copy, bullet-list treatment, and media-embed treatment.
5. Apply `uncodixfy` constraints and reject generic AI UI patterns.
6. Prefer an ivory-light canvas, thin separators, mono metadata, restrained orange markers, and text-led structure.
7. Do not invent hero imagery when no source image exists.
8. Keep the left rail non-sticky and limited to the back link plus the table of contents.
9. Place the orange article marker in the inter-column gap and keep the body copy aligned to the content edge to the right of that marker.

## Done when

- The detail-page layout is explicit.
- The feed-item treatment is explicit.
- Typography and accent usage are explicit.
- The shared-header, reduced-rail, non-sticky TOC rail, inter-column marker, and media-embed rules are explicit.
- The approved article design cues are explicit enough to reuse later without reopening the reference images.
- The design contract is concrete enough to implement without improvisation.
